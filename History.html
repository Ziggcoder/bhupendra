The Taleteller

Cheriyal Scroll painting was started by my Ancestors and followed by my Great Grand Father Late Lakshamaiyah, we belong to the Dhanalakota. The craft was so familiar to them for which they use to locate during their visits to siddipet and sometimes to cheriyal at that time they use to narrate stories from the scrolls which were commissioned by the storytellers who were the traditional customers of Nakash, after his demise, the legacy was carry forwarded by my Grandfather Late Venkatramaiyah, he also did the same job what his father told him at that time all of our family members use to work together. I was born and bought up between these natural colors and brushes. My action shows how eager I was to learn the art when I was 3, I used to make drawings with a stick on the floor then my father Late Chandraiyiah noticed that flourishing art in a child who ends up eating mud every day from the top of the painting, he never taught me the way to do it properly. I learned it by myself from him by continuously watching him making drawings, I started learning cheriyal painting when I was 5 years old, firstly I started with the sketches. Then gradually I was promoted to learn the painting part stated Mr. D. Venkata Ramana

PROCESS

Preparation of Canvas

Fabric cutting:
The artist cuts the khadi fabric to fit the size of the artwork.

Preparation of paste:
The paste is made by combining water and tamarind seed powder in a container and boiling it for 15 minutes.

Adding other ingredients:
The prepared tamarind paste is then mixed with tree gum, chalk powder, rice starch, and Fevicol.

Application of the paste:
To give the khadi fabric rigidity, it is coated with 2 to 3 coats of tamarind paste with a little piece of cloth.

Drying the fabric:
The fabric is dried to give it stretchiness.

Process of Painting

The initial drawing is done with a pencil.

The background is painted (mostly red) except the characters using a goat hair brush.

The artisan then uses a goat hair brush to fill in the colors of the characters.

The outlines and the details are done with black color using fine squirrel hair brushes. The borders are then painted with swirls, curves, stylized flowers, foliage, and other elements.
